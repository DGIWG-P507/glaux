DGIWG GLAUX

Operationalizing NATO STANAG 4789 & OGC API - Connected Systems for Joint ISR Operations

Vision & Mandate

Conceived during the DGIWG Spring 2026 sessions in Athens, Greece, Glaux delivers an open-source suite of reference implementations and ecosystem software. Its primary mission is to provide the resources, evaluation tooling, and implementation guidance necessary to accelerate plug-and-play sensor interoperability across the Alliance.

🏛️ The Significance of Glaux

In classical Greek, Glaux (γλαύξ) refers to the Little Owl that traditionally accompanies Athena, the goddess of strategy, craft, and intelligence. Unlike Ares, who personified chaotic conflict, Athena represented organized defense and Techne—the practical mastery of systematic execution.

The selection of Glaux as the moniker for this DGIWG reference implementation carries a profound dual significance for modern coalition operations:

  • Vision Through the Darkness: The owl is renowned for its luminous, night-seeing eyes, possessing acute perception where others see only noise and obscurity. For a Connected Systems server, this is a flawless operational parallel: the software functions as the central intelligence node, absorbing raw data streams across disparate networks and translating them into a clear, actionable operational picture.
  • The International Standard of Exchange: In antiquity, the silver tetradrachm coins minted in Athens were universally stamped with the Glaux and colloquially known across the Mediterranean simply as "owls." These coins became the trusted, authoritative standard of currency for trade, diplomacy, and alliance across the civilized world.

Conceived on the historic slopes of Athens during the DGIWG Spring 2026 sessions—overlooking the Acropolis and the Areopagus—DGIWG Glaux honors this heritage. It is engineered to be the definitive, trusted baseline for international interoperability, bringing absolute vision, clarity, and standardized harmony to modern Joint ISR sensor networks.
